Closed Meeting Preamble

Hi, I'm _____ and I'm an alcoholic.

Welcome to the Wednesday night men's meeting of the Cosmopolitan Group, both in Little Rock and abroad.

This is a closed meeting of Alcoholics Anonymous. In support of A.A.'s singleness of purpose, attendance at closed meetings is limited to persons who have a desire to stop drinking. If you think you have a problem with alcohol, you are welcome to attend this meeting. We ask that when discussing our problems, we confine ourselves to those problems as they relate to alcoholism.

Since this is a closed meeting, if any of you are in a public place or around non-alcoholics, some here might appreciate you using headphones. To minimize background noise, I've muted everyone, but you can un-mute yourself at any time. Remember to do that when sharing.

Please join me in a moment of silence, followed by the Serenity prayer.

Alcoholics Anonymous is a fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength and hope with each other that they may solve their common problem and help others to recover from alcoholism.

The only requirement for membership is a desire to stop drinking. There are no dues or fees for A.A. membership; we are self-supporting through our own contributions. A.A. is not allied with any sect, denomination, politics, organization or institution; does not wish to engage in any controversy, neither endorses nor opposes any causes. Our primary purpose is to stay sober and help other alcoholics to achieve sobriety.
